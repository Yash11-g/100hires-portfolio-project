Executive Summary

In this session, Armand Farrokh shares practical techniques that help B2B sales professionals improve discovery conversations, handle objections with confidence, and guide opportunities through the sales process more effectively. Rather than relying on persuasion alone, he emphasizes structured conversations, preparation, and understanding customer business problems before recommending solutions.

The discussion reinforces a recurring theme across Armand’s educational content: consistent execution of sales fundamentals produces better long-term results than relying on improvisation or aggressive selling.

⸻

Key Concepts

1. Discovery Creates Better Deals

Strong discovery conversations uncover business priorities, decision-making processes, and the real impact of customer challenges before presenting a solution.

⸻

2. Objections Should Be Explored

Instead of immediately overcoming objections, sales professionals should understand why prospects feel uncertain and use thoughtful follow-up questions to continue the conversation.

⸻

3. Qualification Prevents Wasted Time

Not every opportunity deserves equal attention. Early qualification helps sales teams prioritize high-potential prospects.

⸻

4. Preparation Improves Confidence

Researching prospects before meetings enables more relevant conversations and demonstrates professionalism.

⸻

5. Sales Is a Repeatable Process

High-performing sellers rely on structured frameworks, continuous practice, and consistent execution rather than improvisation.

⸻

Practical Applications

Cold Emails

* Personalize messaging using business context.
* Focus on customer challenges before product capabilities.

Discovery Calls

* Ask layered follow-up questions.
* Identify business impact instead of accepting surface-level answers.

Objection Handling

* Slow down conversations.
* Understand the concern before responding.

Pipeline Generation

* Prioritize qualified opportunities.
* Build repeatable sales processes that can be refined over time.
